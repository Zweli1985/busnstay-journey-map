About Us

Community Gathering

Khanya Community Radio, a non-profit project launched in 1998, received its broadcasting license in November 2000. Based in Mnquma Local Municipality, Eastern Cape, South Africa, we serve Butterworth, Kei Mouth, Kentani, Willowvale, Idutywa, Tsomo, Ngqamakhwe, parts of Elliotdale, and parts of Engcobo, covering municipalities like Mnquma, Mbhashe, Engcobo, and Ntsika Yethu. With a listenership of over 150,000 (RAMS Research, SAARF), we command a strong following and are available globally via live streaming on 106.1 FM.

Our Objectives

  • Engage in educational programs for schools and post-school society.
  • Promote community and high moral values.
  • Encourage dialogue among community members on matters affecting them.
  • Promote socio-economic development, investment, productivity, job creation, and poverty eradication.

Our Mission

Communicate values and standards to the broader community footprint in Mnquma Local Municipality, educating and updating ideas to assist in the development of this predominantly rural community.

Opportunities & Achievements

  • Created opportunities for young people.
  • Unearthed talent in various skills.
  • Exposed young local artists to national events and platforms.
  • Provided opportunities to local presenters on national broadcasting.
