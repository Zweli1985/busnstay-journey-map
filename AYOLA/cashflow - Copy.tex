\section{Assumptions and Basis of Preparation}
\begin{sectionbox}
These cash flow projections have been prepared in accordance with \textbf{IFRS for SMEs (Section 7: Statement of Cash Flows)} as adopted in South Africa via SAICA guidelines.

Key assumptions:
\begin{itemize}
\item Revenue growth of 30\% per annum from Year 2 onward.
\item Payroll costs increasing in line with staff expansion and operational scale.
\item Asset Assist grant of R250,000 received in February 2026.
\item Initial equipment acquisition of R238,305 in February 2026.
\item Vehicles purchased in 2028 (R2,000,000) and property in 2030 (R4,000,000), funded internally.
\item No borrowings, leases, interest, dividends, or income tax cash flows assumed.
\end{itemize}
\end{sectionbox}

\section{Revenue Projections and Growth Analysis}
\begin{sectionbox}
The following revenue projections are based on confirmed service offerings enabled by the Asset Assist-funded equipment. Projections reflect conservative market penetration within the Eastern Cape, gradual national expansion, and diversified income streams to reduce risk.

Revenue streams include:
\begin{itemize}
\item Multimedia & AV Production (corporate videos, podcasts, livestreams)
\item Event Sound, Stage & Hybrid Event Services
\item Digital Marketing, Branding & Content Retainers
\item Enterprise Development, Training & Funding Support
\end{itemize}
\end{sectionbox}

\subsection{Revenue Growth Assumptions}
\begin{sectionbox}
\begin{itemize}
\item Year 1 reflects partial-year capacity ramp-up following asset acquisition.
\item Year 2 growth is driven by full utilisation of equipment, repeat clients, and retainers.
\item Year 3 includes expanded corporate contracts, national events, and higher-margin premium services.
\item Average annual revenue growth of approximately 30\% is assumed.
\end{itemize}
\end{sectionbox}
